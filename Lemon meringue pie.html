Lemon Meringue Pie

A golden, flaky pie crust filled with smooth, tangy lemon curd and topped with a cloud of toasted meringue — a classic balance of sweet and tart perfection.

Prep Time: 25 mins

Bake Time: 30 mins

Servings: 8

Ingredients

For the Pie Crust:

  • 1 1/4 cups (160g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 115g (1/2 cup) cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 3–4 tbsp cold water

For the Lemon Filling:

  • 1 cup (200g) sugar
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 cup (240ml) water
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp lemon zest
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) fresh lemon juice
  • Pinch of salt

For the Meringue:

  • 3 large egg whites
  • 1/4 tsp cream of tartar
  • 1/3 cup (70g) sugar
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pie Crust: In a bowl, combine flour, sugar, and salt. Cut in butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Add cold water, one tablespoon at a time, until the dough just comes together.
  2. Form into a disk, wrap, and chill for 30 minutes. Roll out and fit into a 9-inch pie dish. Prick the base with a fork and blind-bake at 180°C (350°F) for 15 minutes with pie weights, then 10 minutes without, until golden.
  3. Make the Lemon Filling: In a saucepan, whisk together sugar, cornstarch, and salt. Gradually whisk in water, then cook over medium heat until thick and bubbly.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk egg yolks. Slowly pour a bit of the hot mixture into the yolks to temper, then return to the saucepan. Cook for another 1–2 minutes until thick. Remove from heat and stir in butter, lemon juice, and zest.
  5. Pour the hot filling into the baked crust and smooth the surface.
  6. Make the Meringue: In a clean bowl, beat egg whites and cream of tartar until foamy. Gradually add sugar and beat until glossy stiff peaks form. Beat in vanilla extract.
  7. Spread the meringue over the hot filling, sealing the edges completely to prevent shrinking.
  8. Bake: Return the pie to the oven and bake at 180°C (350°F) for 10–12 minutes, until the meringue is golden brown.
  9. Cool completely before slicing. Best served slightly chilled with coffee or tea.
